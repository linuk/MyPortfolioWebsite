State 3 ways in which your site is accessible.


  • Aria-label
    I added aria-labels to every component which need it, such as links in navigation bar, icons in the about page and input areas in the form of contact page, all of these labels make the screen reader never miss any of those important components, and allow blind or visually impaired users access to my website easier.

  • Contrast control
    After browsing contrastrebellion.com, I decided to slightly change the color scheme of my web page. Based on WCAG of contrast, I updated the colors and managed the contrast ratio to at least 4.5: 1, which reach AA level and improve the readability.

  • Alts
    A part of my website contains photos and I added alt attributes to them. These allow users access to the information of the images, no matter they use the screen reader or the brwoser fail to load the images.

State 3 ways in which you have considered usability in your site.


  • I've considered following three usabilities during my deisn based on Danish web usability consultant Jakob Nielsen's 10 general principles for interaction design.

  • Visibility of system status
    All of the clickable links, buttons and images have hover effects, which can give the users feedbacks that the components are clickable. And there is no unnecessary hover effects to confuse users either. Besides, the users can always know which page they are brwosing thanks to the navigation bar hightlight feature.

  • Match between system and the real world
    Except the index page, each page contain only one card and users can easily browse and focus on it. The navigation bar is always on the top whole time, also it follows the modern website convention, back to index page by clicking logo and links in navigation are aligned to right.

  • Aesthetic and minimalist design
    Withouth redundent design and information, I simplified the contents and organized them in different pages and users can easily find what they want to see via the navigation bar. Users will be provided just enough information and they can read more by simply clicking corresponding icon, image or link.
